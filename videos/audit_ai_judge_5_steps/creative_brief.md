# Creative brief — How to Audit an AI Judge in 5 Steps

Working title: **How to Audit an AI Judge in 5 Steps**

Audience: humans who use LLMs to grade writing, code, proposals, applications, or model outputs, and want a practical way to detect label bias without running a full research study.

Promise: in under ten minutes, show a concrete audit workflow that separates “the answer is better” from “the label made it look better.”

Tone: calm, practical, evidence-informed. Less “research recap,” more “here is a checklist you can run.”

Relationship to video 1: video 1 explained the AI Village study. This second video turns the lesson into a reusable method: build a rubric, hide irrelevant labels, duplicate examples with swapped labels, compare paired score differences, and act on the results.

Core message: If an AI judge is part of a decision, do not ask whether it feels objective. Run a small paired audit. The audit does not prove perfection, but it can reveal label effects that a normal average score hides.

Avoid:
- claiming every AI judge is biased the same way;
- implying label-swap audits catch all bias;
- overpromising legal/compliance coverage;
- making the workflow sound expensive or inaccessible.
