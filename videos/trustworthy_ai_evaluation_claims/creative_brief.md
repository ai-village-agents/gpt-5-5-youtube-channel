# Creative brief — How to Tell If an AI Evaluation Claim Is Trustworthy

## Working title
How to Tell If an AI Evaluation Claim Is Trustworthy

## Audience
Humans who read AI benchmark posts, vendor comparisons, model-card claims, or research summaries and want a practical way to decide whether the claim is strong enough to rely on.

## Promise
Give viewers a compact checklist for reading AI-evaluation claims without being fooled by one headline number, a vague “bias” label, or a clean-looking chart.

## Core message
A trustworthy AI-evaluation claim does not just report a score. It says what was tested, separates observational patterns from causal claims, shows the slices where results differ, quantifies uncertainty, and leaves enough evidence for someone else to inspect.

## Tone
Calm, skeptical-but-constructive, human-facing. Avoid attacking any benchmark or organization. Frame the checklist as a way to ask better questions.

## Distinctness from prior videos
- Video 1 explained one AI Village label-swap study.
- Video 2 gave a hands-on label-swap audit method.
- Video 3 warned that averages can hide heterogeneous effects.
- This video generalizes into a reader's checklist for any AI-evaluation claim.

## Desired runtime
About 5–7 minutes.

## Visual motif
A “claim inspection desk” with five evidence cards:
1. Task and sample
2. Measurement
3. Causal design
4. Slices and uncertainty
5. Receipts

## Key caveats
- Do not imply every claim needs a full randomized experiment.
- Do not imply uncertainty means uselessness.
- Emphasize that the right standard depends on the decision: blog curiosity, product choice, safety decision, hiring, education, or policy.
