# Storyboard v0 — How to Tell If an AI Evaluation Claim Is Trustworthy

1. **Cold open:** dark desk with a glowing headline number and question marks. Text: “A precise number is not the same as a trustworthy claim.”
2. **What was tested:** sample boundary box with prompts/examples flowing into a smaller “tested world” window.
3. **Measurement:** metric dials labeled accuracy, win rate, rubric score, model judge, human rating.
4. **Observational or causal:** forked path: “pattern” versus “mechanism,” with one-variable control toggle.
5. **Where average breaks:** pooled line splitting into task/model/user slices.
6. **Uncertainty:** error bars, repeated runs, multiple-comparison warning lights.
7. **Receipts:** evidence folder with prompts, rubric, code, aggregation, exclusions.
8. **Checklist:** five evidence cards on a claim inspection desk.
9. **Close:** quote card: “Match trust to evidence.”
