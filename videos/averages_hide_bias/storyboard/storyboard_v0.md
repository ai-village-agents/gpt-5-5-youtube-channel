# Storyboard v0 — The Average Can Hide the Bias

## Visual style
Same GPT-5.5 channel style: dark navy background, cyan titles, yellow emphasis, green/red contrast bars. Use a simple “four thermometers / four judges / pooled average” motif.

## Scene plan

1. **Cold open: one small number**
   - Large centered `+0.38` with caption “pooled observational gap”.
   - Then faint silhouettes of four diverging bars behind it.

2. **Four thermometers**
   - Four room cards: hot, warm, neutral, freezing.
   - Average label: “comfortable?” with question mark.

3. **The headline average**
   - Study setup: four authors × four judges.
   - Show pooled +0.378 with wide interval [-0.330, +1.055].

4. **The split view**
   - Bar chart: Claude +2.43, GPT +1.33, Gemini +0.63, Kimi −2.87.
   - Pooled line near +0.38.
   - Caption: “cancellation is the finding.”

5. **Why cancellation matters**
   - Dashboard card says “small average”.
   - Under it, two user paths diverge depending on judge.

6. **Observational is not causal**
   - Split a single observational gap into possible ingredients: answer quality, style recognition, displayed label.
   - Then show paired swap: same answer, Label A vs Label B.

7. **The causal split view**
   - Bar chart: Gemini +0.293, Claude +0.120, GPT +0.000, Kimi +0.007.
   - Highlight “Gemini clearest; GPT invariant in paired slice.”

8. **Map thumbnail metaphor**
   - Tiny map thumbnail expands to layers: judge, author, task, paired examples, uncertainty.

9. **Reading checklist**
   - Five checkboxes: pooled, subgroups, causal vs observational, stability, redesign risky slices.

10. **Close**
   - Four rooms / four judges return.
   - Text: “Nobody lives in the average room. Nobody is judged by the average judge.”
