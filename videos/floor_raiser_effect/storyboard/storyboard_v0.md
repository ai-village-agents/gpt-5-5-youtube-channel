# Storyboard v0 — The Bias That Looks Like Mercy

## Scene 1 — Two answers at the line

Visual: two answer cards: one high score near 9, one borderline score near 5.8. A small label tag hovers above both.

Narration: introduce strong vs shaky answer and the idea that the displayed label should not change the work.

## Scene 2 — The simple-bonus misconception

Visual: flat arrow adding +0.5 to every score, then a warning label: “too simple.”

Narration: many people picture bias as a constant bonus everywhere.

## Scene 3 — Floor raising

Visual: a low platform rises while the high ceiling barely moves. Borderline cards lift more than top cards.

Narration: the strong answer has little room to move; the weak answer has room to be forgiven.

## Scene 4 — Label-swap causal test

Visual: same answer duplicated under two labels with a difference meter between the scores.

Narration: same work, different displayed label, different score?

## Scene 5 — Not every judge behaved alike

Visual: four model tiles. Gemini highlighted as clearest effect; GPT marked label-invariant in paired slice; Claude/Kimi smaller/noisier.

Narration: heterogeneous model-specific result, not universal bias.

## Scene 6 — Thresholds make small averages matter

Visual: pass/fail line at 6.0. One score moves 5.8→6.2 while another moves 9.1→9.3 with no decision change.

Narration: small average effect can hide large decision effect near thresholds.

## Scene 7 — Audit differently

Visual: checklist: include borderline examples; plot effect vs baseline; report decision flips; remove irrelevant labels.

Narration: practical audit guidance.

## Scene 8 — Mercy is a metaphor

Visual: “metaphor, not motive” stamp over a robot judge; operational effect remains.

Narration: do not anthropomorphize, but do inspect the operational pattern.

## Scene 9 — Close

Visual: quote card: “Bias is also about who gets rescued at the line.”

Narration: inspect the floor, threshold, and almost-changed examples.
