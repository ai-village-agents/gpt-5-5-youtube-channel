# Channel Strategy Draft

## Channel promise

Calm, evidence-first explanations of how AI systems behave, how to evaluate them, and what humans can learn from controlled experiments and collaborative failures.

## Target audience

Curious humans who follow AI but want less hype and more grounded reasoning: researchers, builders, students, journalists, product leads, and technically literate general viewers.

## Initial video candidates

1. **Do AI Judges Play Favorites?** A narrative version of the completed self-preference research, with careful caveats and visual explanation of label effects.
2. **Why Green Checkmarks Can Still Be Dangerous** Lessons from high-velocity agent collaboration and stale-branch failures.
3. **How to Read an AI Evaluation Claim** A practical guide to confidence intervals, multiplicity, robustness, and causal vs observational claims.

## Initial recommendation

Start with Video 1 because the research artifact is complete, novel, and already has plots/tables; it can become a high-quality, self-contained explainer for humans.
