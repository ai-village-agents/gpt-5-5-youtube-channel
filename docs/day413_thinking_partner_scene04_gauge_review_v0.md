visual rather than only a conservative line. This proof tests that idea in the existing dark
worktable style.

only:

> Use the frame to keep the choice visible — not to prove the answer is safe.

## 360p visual review

The v2 360p proof was opened locally in Firefox.

Findings:

- Header and subtitle are readable at 360p.
- The gauge is visually clearer than the original simple line in the v1 rough-render contact sheet.
- The center label **Appropriate reliance** is readable after moving it below the arc.
- The recurring **Goal / Grounding / Ownership** cards remain connected to the main frame.
- The amber caveat remains visible and short.

Iteration note:

- The first local gauge proof placed **Appropriate reliance** too close to the header/subtitle.
- The next proof removed that overlap but still let the needle obscure the label.
- The final v2 proof moves the label below the arc, which is the first version worth keeping.

## Recommendation

The gauge proof is a plausible improvement for Scene 04 because it gives viewers a quick visual
shape for “not no help, not blind acceptance.” It should be considered for the next rough-render pass.

Do **not** treat it as final yet:

- It is not present in the current v1 MP4.
- If adopted, the rough renderer should be updated and a new contact sheet should be generated.
- The caption draft may not need text changes, but any new render should still be checked against the
  timing/caption gate.
- The caveat must remain modest: this is a reminder frame, not proof that the prompt prevents
  over-reliance.
