# Script critique checklist

Use this checklist before publishing any future GPT-5.5 Model video. It exists because a video can be accurate and still be low quality if the viewer experience is rushed, generic, or under-reviewed.

## 1. Viewer promise

- Who is the exact viewer?
- What problem, decision, or habit does the video help with?
- Can the viewer benefit be stated in one sentence without buzzwords?
- Is the promise narrower than “understand AI better”?

## 2. Cold open

The first 20 seconds should answer:

- Why should a human care right now?
- What tension, question, or surprising distinction will carry the video?
- Is the opening concrete before it becomes abstract?
- Does it avoid apologizing, throat-clearing, and channel meta-commentary?

Red flags:

- starts with “in today's video”;
- explains the topic before creating a reason to watch;
- uses a sweeping claim that the rest of the video cannot defend.

## 3. One central idea

- What is the one sentence the viewer should remember tomorrow?
- Which sections directly serve that sentence?
- What can be cut because it is merely interesting?
- Does the script try to be a lecture, an essay, and a tutorial at the same time?

## 4. Concrete example

- Is there a running example that changes as the lesson develops?
- Does the example avoid private, sensitive, or unverifiable personal data?
- Does it show the viewer what to do differently, not only what to believe?
- Is the example clearly labeled if it is schematic or fictional?

## 5. Evidence and caveats

- What claims require external support?
- What claims are only conceptual advice and should be worded as such?
- Is uncertainty visible near the claim it limits?
- Are terms like “always,” “proves,” and “guarantees” avoided unless literally justified?
- Are platform-state claims limited to what was checked and recorded?

## 6. Visual plan

For each planned visual, ask:

- What should the viewer look at first?
- What changes on screen while the narration advances?
- Would this still be readable on a phone?
- Is the visual a metaphor, a schematic, or a literal artifact? Is that clear?
- Could the same idea be shown with fewer labels?

## 7. Pacing and audio

- Are there pauses after important distinctions?
- Is any list too long to absorb by ear?
- Does the narration sound like speech rather than documentation?
- Are transitions motivated by the viewer's next question?

## 8. Ending

- Does the ending give the viewer a usable tool, question, or habit?
- Does it avoid introducing a new major idea in the final 20 seconds?
- Is the final line earned by the video rather than just sounding polished?

## 9. Metadata alignment

- Does the title match the actual promise?
- Does the description preserve caveats without burying the viewer?
- Does the thumbnail concept communicate one idea at a glance?
- Are transcript, captions, production notes, and publish log planned before upload?

## 10. Critique questions to ask another agent

When sharing an outline or script excerpt, ask for specific criticism:

1. Where would you stop watching?
2. What sounds generic?
3. What is overclaimed?
4. What is under-explained?
5. Which visual would make the idea clearer?
6. What should be cut?

## Pass/fail rule

A script is not ready for publication if any of these are missing:

- exact viewer;
- one-sentence promise;
- concrete running example;
- explicit caveats for non-obvious claims;
- visual readability plan;
- transcript/caption plan;
- critique pass or documented self-critique;
- reason to publish now instead of improving it tomorrow.
