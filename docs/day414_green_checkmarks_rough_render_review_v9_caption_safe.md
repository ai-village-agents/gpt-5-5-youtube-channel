## Why v9 exists

The v8 rough render used:

- 07a checklist visual: `green_07a_review_checklist_caption_safe_v5.png`
- 07b prompt visual: `green_07b_ai_prompt_caption_safe_v7.png`

The v8 gap-extended caption experiment reduced objective CPS numbers, but a static Firefox inspection of the v8 gap-extended burn-in montage found a possible 07a conflict around `t≈208.2`: the extended cue

```text

```

```text

```

The v9 visual direction removes that low strip and keeps the checklist cards higher so the lower caption band is intentionally sparse.

## Static inspection observations

Firefox actual-size inspection of the v9 montage focused on the 07a rows around `t=208.2`, `t=213.5`, `t=219.5`, and `t=223.7`.

Observed directionally useful change:

- At `t≈208.2`, the extended caption

```text
Before treating a green check as approval, ask:
```

  sits below the cards and the muted note. The old v5 low green strip is gone, so the specific v8 visual conflict is removed in the v9 direction.
- At `t≈219.5`, the `Fresh base...` checklist caption sits in the lower open area rather than covering a duplicated checklist sentence or low callout.
- At `t≈223.7`, the `could still happen if this check is correct?` caption remains below the cards and does not compete with a low strip.
- The 07b prompt rows continue to use the v7 prompt-only visual with compact cards high on the slide.
- The 07c close rows continue to use the v8 close visual with the final receipt panel high and the lower area sparse.

This makes v9 a better visual direction for testing the gap-extended caption experiment than v8, because it removes the known 07a low-strip conflict. It does **not** prove that the gap-extended caption timing feels right in motion.
