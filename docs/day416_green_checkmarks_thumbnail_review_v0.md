## Concept

The thumbnail follows the current metadata dry-run direction from
`day416_green_checkmarks_metadata_dry_run_v0.md`.

Main text:

- `GREEN CHECK`
- `≠ VERDICT`

Support text / visual idea:

- three small review chips: **fresh base**, **right diff**, **risk left**;
- a receipt-like green check card on the left, with `version?`, `diff?`, and `risk?` lines;
- bottom callout: `Read the receipt before approval`;
- no PR numbers, agent names, panic framing, or claim that automated checks are useless.

## Strengths

- The promise matches the script: a check is evidence to read, not an approval verdict.
- The main phrase remains legible at 640×360.
- The three-part habit is present without requiring the thumbnail to carry the full lesson.
- The 640×360 review asset no longer clips the third support chip.
- The receipt visual aligns with the title and final line of the current candidate.
- It avoids forbidden or overstrong claims such as `CI MISSED EVERYTHING` or `green checks are dangerous`.

## Concerns

- The support chips are readable in the 640×360 asset, but they are secondary; a very small phone preview may reduce them to texture.
- The third chip uses the shorter phrase `risk left` rather than the canonical script phrase `uncovered risk`; this is a thumbnail legibility compromise, not a script change.
- The phrase `≠ VERDICT` is accurate, but if the symbol reads poorly in some YouTube surfaces, a future variant could test `NOT A VERDICT`.
- The thumbnail is developer-facing and calm; it may be less clickable than a more dramatic concept, but it better matches the source-caveated script.
- This is a generated concept asset only. Prior channel work found custom thumbnail upload may require phone verification, so this must not be described as live.

## Possible v1 improvements

1. Compare `GREEN CHECK ≠ VERDICT` against `NOT A VERDICT` for symbol legibility.
2. Test a simpler support line: `FRESH BASE / RIGHT DIFF / UNCOVERED RISK`.
3. Create a contact sheet against one frame from the actual opening to ensure visual continuity.
4. If a future upload becomes realistic, verify how the thumbnail appears inside YouTube Studio rather than relying only on local PNG review.
