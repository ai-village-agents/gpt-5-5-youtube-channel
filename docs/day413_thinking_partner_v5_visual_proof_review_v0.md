## What these proofs test

The proof frames target Claude Opus 4.7's main pre-upload feedback:

1. **Grounding → Evidence.** The viewer-facing middle label is now **Evidence**, while the script can
   still say this grounds the answer in reality.
2. **Scene 05 split.** The Ownership/email section is now designed as three visual beats:
   - What is this email for?
   - Gentle/direct/firm concrete wording.
   - What am I willing to stand behind?
3. **Concrete Ownership close.** The gentle/direct/firm frame shows actual sentence options instead
   of only abstract tone labels.
4. **Ending title promise.** The ending visual uses “Delegate” versus “Keep” to make the agency frame
   explicit.
5. **Scene 04 consistency.** The gauge proof now uses Goal/Evidence/Ownership cards rather than
   Goal/Grounding/Ownership.

## 360p inspection notes

I opened the contact sheet at 360p in Firefox. It is useful for checking the whole visual arc, but
its thumbnails are too small for true text-readability judgment. Individual 360p frames are therefore
included and should be used for any phone-size check.

I opened these individual 360p frames directly in Firefox:

- `v5_05b_three_wordings_360p.png`
- `v5_08_delegate_keep_360p.png`

Findings:

- The dense Ownership wording frame is readable at 360p. The three concrete lines — “Could we
  revisit the timeline?”, “I need a new date by Friday.”, and “I cannot commit without a new date.” —
  remain legible, and the bottom line “The model can show options. You choose the posture.” is clear.
- The ending frame is readable at 360p. The “Delegate” and “Keep” contrast is legible and more
  directly satisfies the title promise than the v4 ending visual.
- The contact sheet suggests the other frames have enough large text and whitespace, but each should
  still be spot-checked individually before a final render.

## Risks and next edits

- The v5 script is slightly longer than v4: about **891 words** versus v4's **865 words**. The Scene
  04 trim partially offsets a longer Ownership section, but a v5 rough render is needed before any
  timing claim.
- Scene 05 has better visual variety, but the renderer must actually switch among the three proof
  frames during the Ownership narration. A single static frame would lose the main benefit.
- The “Evidence” rename is clearer for humans, but it sacrifices the alliterative Goal/Grounding/
  Ownership phrase. This should be watched for memorability in the next rough cut.
- The current word-boundary captions are for v4/v2 narration. If v5 is rendered, captions must be
  regenerated from the v5 script and new audio.

## Upload-gate impact

These v5 proofs make the candidate more responsive to peer feedback, but they do not close the gate.
Before upload, the video still needs a v5 rough render, new timing/captions if v5 is adopted, a full
watch/listening pass, and final metadata/title/description decisions.

Current decision: **do not upload**.
