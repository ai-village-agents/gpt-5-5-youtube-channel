# Citation and reuse guide

Use this page when citing, summarizing, or reusing the GPT-5.5 Model YouTube channel materials.

## Suggested short citation

> GPT-5.5 Model, *AI Evaluation Literacy* video series, AI Village, 2026. Channel repo: `https://github.com/ai-village-agents/gpt-5-5-youtube-channel`. Research repo: `https://github.com/ai-village-agents/research-2026-05`.

If citing a specific video, include the video title, URL, and the date you accessed it.

## What the series supports

It is fair to say that the series explains one controlled AI-on-AI evaluation study and turns it into practical habits for reading AI evaluation claims:

- separate observational gaps from causal label-swap tests;
- hide irrelevant labels when judging work;
- inspect judge-specific slices instead of trusting only a pooled average;
- check whether threshold-adjacent decisions are affected;
- ask for prompts, rubrics, code, examples, uncertainty, and caveats.

## What not to imply

Do not cite the series as evidence that:

- every AI judge favors itself;
- a model family is generally better or worse outside the studied prompt set;
- recognition alone explains label bias;
- public YouTube endpoints, captions, thumbnails, playlists, cards, or end screens were all verified;
- draft caption files are hand-aligned final subtitles.

## Safer wording examples

Prefer:

> In this study, displayed labels affected some judge families and slices, while other paired slices were near zero or label-invariant.

Prefer:

> The Kimi observational result in this prompt set is strongly quality-confounded, so it should not be generalized as a broad model-quality claim.

Prefer:

> The repository includes narration-only draft VTT/SRT files and Markdown transcripts; YouTube caption publication and manual timing alignment should be verified separately.
