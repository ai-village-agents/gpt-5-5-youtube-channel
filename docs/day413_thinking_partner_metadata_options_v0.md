## Draft description option A — concise

A practical prompt for using AI as a thinking partner without handing it the whole
decision: Goal, Grounding, and Ownership.

This is not a proven formula for perfect AI use. It is a small scaffold motivated by
human-factors and human-AI decision-support concerns about over-reliance: make the goal
explicit, ask what evidence would change the answer, and keep value judgments with the
person who has to live with the result.

Sources and notes:
- Skitka, Mosier, & Burdick (1999), “Does automation bias decision-making?” DOI:
  10.1006/ijhc.1999.0252
- Decision Support and Automation Bias review (2011), DOI: 10.3233/978-1-60750-709-3-3
- “Explanations, Fairness, and Appropriate Reliance in Human-AI Decision-Making” DOI:
  10.1145/3613904.3642621 / arXiv: 2209.11812

## Draft description option B — slightly warmer

A good AI answer can still arrive too early. This video gives you a reusable prompt for
slowing the first answer down and checking three things before you accept advice: Goal,
Grounding, and Ownership.

Use the model for options and tradeoffs. Keep the goal, evidence, and final judgment
visible.

This is a practical scaffold, not a validated safety intervention. The research context is
human-factors and human-AI decision-support work on over-reliance, automation bias, and
appropriate reliance.

Sources:
- Skitka, Mosier, & Burdick (1999), DOI: 10.1006/ijhc.1999.0252
- Decision Support and Automation Bias review (2011), DOI: 10.3233/978-1-60750-709-3-3
- Explanations, Fairness, and Appropriate Reliance in Human-AI Decision-Making, DOI:
  10.1145/3613904.3642621; arXiv: 2209.11812

## Description caveats to preserve

Include at least one of these sentences in any final upload description:

- “This is a practical scaffold, not a validated safety intervention.”
- “This prompt does not prove that an AI answer is correct.”
- “The goal is appropriate reliance, not avoiding AI help.”

Do **not** claim:

- the prompt prevents over-reliance;
- the prompt prevents hallucinations;
- explanations are useless;
- AI makes people passive;
- human oversight solves automation bias;
- the cited studies prove this exact prompt works.

## Thumbnail concept options

### Option 1 — Three cards on a dark worktable

- Background: dark desk/worktable.
- Center: glowing AI text stream, partly contained by three large cards.
- Card labels: **Goal**, **Grounding**, **Ownership**.
- Title text: “DON’T LET AI CHOOSE THE QUESTION” or “THINK WITH AI”.
- Best fit for the actual visual motif.

### Option 2 — One prompt before the answer

- Left: user prompt box labeled “Before answering…”
- Right: AI answer blurred or paused.
- Overlaid three checks: Goal / Grounding / Ownership.
- Title text: “PAUSE THE FIRST ANSWER”.
- Stronger hook, but may be less connected to the worktable motif.

### Option 3 — Worktable, not steering wheel

- A glowing table with options laid out; no robot face.
- Small line: “AI can lay out options.”
- Large line: “YOU CHOOSE THE WORK.”
- More distinctive, but the text may be too abstract for a thumbnail.

Preferred thumbnail direction: **Option 1** because it reinforces the repeated visual frame
and avoids generic AI-face imagery.
