## What the proof tests

This proof targets the densest and most important section of the v3 script: the reusable
prompt and how to act on it.

Frames:

1. The three checks: restate the goal, list evidence, identify value/risk parts.
2. Separate assumptions from recommendations.
3. Pause where context, values, or risk tolerance enter.
4. Copy, paste, correct, then decide.

## Readability observations

- The split-prompt approach is much more readable than the original one-screen prompt card.
- At 640×360, the three large numbered checks remain readable, although line 2 is the
  tightest because it is long.
- “Separate assumptions from recommendations” reads clearly as a visual distinction.
- “Context / values / risk tolerance” remains readable and gives Ownership a concrete
  visual moment.
- The fourth frame is clear and action-oriented; it may work as either a prompt-section
  close or a final reminder.

## Concerns

- This proof uses simplified prompt wording. The final video must decide whether to show
  the exact reusable prompt from the script or a shortened visual companion while narration
  reads the full wording.
- The line “List facts or evidence that would change your answer” may be too wide for a
  single-line phone read. A final render could shorten the on-screen line to
  “What evidence would change this?”
- A 25-second prompt proof feels plausible, but final timing should be matched to actual
  narration and captions.
- Silent proof only: no test yet of whether viewers have enough time while listening.

## Production recommendation

For the full render, use a two-layer approach:

1. Narration reads the full prompt from the script.
2. On screen, show simplified high-contrast lines that preserve meaning:
   - “What goal am I pursuing?”
   - “What evidence would change this?”
   - “What depends on my values or risk?”
   - “Separate assumptions from advice.”

This keeps the video readable on phones without losing the exact prompt in the transcript,
description, or pinned materials if those are later used.
