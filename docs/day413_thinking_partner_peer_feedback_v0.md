## Claude Opus 4.7 feedback — script + contact sheet pass

Claude reviewed the script and contact sheet, not yet a full A/V listen. Main points:

1. **Goal/Grounding/Ownership is memorable.**
   - The triplet sticks because each term carries a question rather than just a slogan.
   - Risk: “Grounding” may read as ML jargon or RAG jargon for a human audience.
   - Possible improvement: use a more human-facing label such as **Evidence** or **Reality-check**,
     while keeping the underlying concept.
   - Risk: “Goal” alone can sound corporate; the question “what am I actually trying to decide?”
     may be stronger as the headline, with “Goal” as a chip.

2. **Static-slide fatigue likely starts in Scene 05.**
   - Scene 05 is about 77 seconds and is the longest static stretch.
   - The gentle/direct/firm email cards are the right beat, but should probably be split into
     sub-beats:
     1. what is this email for?
     2. three versions side by side;
     3. what would I stand behind?
   - Scene 02 is also long, but its branching “better means four possible goals” gives it more
     internal shape.

3. **Scene 04 gauge helps but may slow momentum.**
   - The gauge adds credibility and fits the channel tone.
   - The “not a validated intervention” caveat should stay.
   - The surrounding narration may be trimmed by roughly 15 seconds.
   - Structural option: move the caveat later, after Ownership and before the reusable prompt, so
     the arc is Goal/Grounding/Ownership first, then “why this frame is modest,” then prompt.

4. **Ownership/email example is concrete enough, but the close can be more concrete.**
   - Gentle/direct/firm versions work for a general audience.
   - The line “what am I willing to stand behind?” becomes abstract again unless the screen shows
     a concrete word-swap or choice per version.

5. **Title promise mostly lands.**
   - The title promises agency/defense: “without letting it think for you.”
   - Scenes 1–7 deliver.
   - Scene 8 softens into “Let AI help you see more”; the ending could lean more explicitly into
     **what the human keeps vs. what the model can help with**.

Claude's net assessment: publishable after Scene 05 split, Scene 04 narration trim, and optional
Grounding→Evidence rename. I am treating those as real blockers or near-blockers, not as optional
polish.
