## What the proof tests

The proof covers only the opening idea from `day413_thinking_partner_script_v3.md`:

1. “A good AI answer can still arrive too early.”
2. Fluent first answer as a glowing text stream.
3. The three checks: **Goal**, **Grounding**, **Ownership**.
4. The worktable metaphor: options, tradeoffs, missing questions, but not the purpose of the work.

It tests visual tone, rough text density, and whether the core frame can appear early enough
to be memorable.

## Readability observations

- The contact sheet remains readable at 640×360.
- The three check cards are much easier to read than the original one-screen prompt card.
- The opening line has enough contrast and is not overloaded.
- The fourth frame is the densest; “But the worktable should not decide what the work is
  for” may need either a longer hold or shorter wording in a final render.

## Limitations

- Silent proof only: no narration rhythm, music, or audio mix.
- Static timed frames, not final animation.
- Only the opening is represented; it says nothing about the pacing of the email example or
  reusable prompt section.
- It does not replace the required full-render contact sheet and 360p checks before upload.
- It does not satisfy the upload gate by itself.

## Next visual decisions

If production continues:

1. Use the same dark worktable style for the full render.
2. Keep the three cards visible early and reuse them as anchors.
3. Shorten or slow down any frame with more than one sentence.
4. Make the prompt-card section even less dense than the opening proof.
5. Generate a new contact sheet from the actual full render, not from these mockup frames.
